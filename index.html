Adol Christin

Adol Christin is the main protagonist of the Ys chronology.

Timeline of Adol Christin Adventures:

  • 0 AC - Adol is born in some unknown small village near unnamed mountain.
  • 17 AC - Adol leave his hometown village and set into a journey. He arrives at Town of Minea after a trouble in his first goal of adventure to Altago due to war. In there, he heard the rumor of an island of vortex called Ystoria. He sails to that place due to his interest in adventure.
  • 17 AC - Adol meets Dogi, his long-time partner in his adventure.
  • 17-18 AC - After revealing the mistery of Ystoria, Adol sets his next goal to Celceta only to be involved in trouble of Normans pirates.
  • 18 AC - Adol arrives in Celceta and clears the mistery of its Great Forest. He also solves the problem of Masks of the Sun, a taboo item that enhaced the power of the user.
  • 19 AC - Adol goes to Dogi's Hometown in Felghana, solving the malevolent of the Felghana Army. He partways with Dogi who wants to help rebuilds his hometown.
  • 20 AC - Adol arrives at the Kingdom of Kefin, in the centre of Xandria desert. Due to his involvement in stoping the ruin of towns because of the desert, The people of Kefin thanks him by making his statue in the middle of the city.
  • 20-21 AC - Adol reunites again with Dogi after the event in Felghana.
  • 21 AC - Adol stranded in the isle of seiren where many ships encounter shipwreck. He discovers the ancient tales of the island.
  • Early 23 AC - Adol stranded in Canaan Island after involved in Romun-Altago war. He discovers the way to release Canaan Island from its eternal vortex by destroying The Ark of Napisthim, the tool to control weather.
  • Late 23 AC - Adol finally arrives in Altago after a temporary ceasefire of Romun-Altago war. He is involved in the investigation of Altago's earthquake phenomenon.
  • 24 AC - After living for sometime in Altago, Adol goes to Balduq only to be thrown into cells due to power struggle inside the Kingdom. He helps to solve Balduq City's problem and its popular legends known as Monstrum.
  • 50-59 AC - Sometime in this period, Adol returns to his hometown and starts writing his travelogue contains his journals of adventure in every area he discovers.
  • 63 AC - Adol completes his travelogue and sets to his final adventures to the North Pole. It is believed he never returns after that adventure and presumed to finally met his end.
